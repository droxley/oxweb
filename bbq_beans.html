Mmmmm…beans!

BBQ beans are one of the all-time great side dishes. How do I make mine? Here’s a guide, and I encourage you to use it as a guide and tweak them to suit your taste and local situation (e.g. what ingredients are available to you).

To start, here’s a link to the first bbq beans video I watched. If you search for "bbq beans” they’re all very similar but to me this is the original:

How to Barbecue Baked Beans | Recipe

So, what do I do?

Ingredients (note measurements are best guesses, amount of beans below is when there’s a large gathering):
8 cans of kidney beans
1 cup ketchup
½ cup yellow mustard
⅓ cup brown sugar
⅓ cup molasses
⅓ cup bbq sauce (store bought or homemade)
Dash of liquid smoke if you’ve got it, or add real smoke or don’t worry about it
400g bacon
2 diced brown onions

A few ingredient notes:
A) If you don’t have molasses (and don’t want to buy a bunch for one bean trial) just use a bit more brown sugar OR you could use maple syrup…I’ve never tried golden syrup but that could be interesting.
B) You can dial back the sweet components if that’s your preference
C) My approach tends to be adding semi-equal amounts of the ketchup, mustard, brown sugar, molasses and bbq sauce at the start then as time goes on and the liquid evaporates I add some more ketchup and/or mustard and/or brown sugar to dial in the taste I’m after, aiming to give the final amounts at least an hour to cook
D) When using 8 cans of beans I usually drain 2 or 3 so there’s a bit less liquid to cook off
E) Obviously leave out the bacon to make it vegetarian
F) Easy to swap the bbq sauce with apple cider vinegar, but I’m more likely to have bbq sauce handy (possibly having used up my apple cider vinegar making bbq sauce)

Instructions:
1) Dice onion and bacon, sauté them both, either in a separate pan or in whatever you’re cooking the beans in (see step 2 below)
2) Dump your beans into whatever you’re cooking in, it should be able to handle low overall heat for a few hours and maybe direct flame (an over safe pot, disposable roaster pan, etc.) and add the onion and bacon and mix
3) Add all the other ingredients (see note C above) and mix everything together
4) Cook for a few hours on low heat (120C or 250F), having a look every 30-45 minutes and giving it a stir, a few different ways to cook:
 a) In an oven: easy to set the temp
 b) In a smoker: lets you add some smoke to the equation, very tasty
 c) On the BBQ (my method): I set it up with indirect heat, usually turning on burner 1 and 4 and putting the roaster pan in between, sometimes I'll put wood chips in a folder foil dish over a burner to add smoke
 d) On the stove: highest risk but can be the quickest, be sure you use the lowest heat you can and check and stir more often so the bottom doesn’t burn!
5) As you’re checking on them, stir them and every now and again taste them, adding ketchup/mustard/brown sugar and such as you’d like, stir it in and check back later to see how it’s going
6) After a few hours the beans should be tender and the liquid reduced, often towards the end I’ll fire up a burner under the roasting pan, set it on low, and boil off the last liquid more quickly to reach the desired thickness
7) Serve them warm and enjoy
8) They’re great as left overs too!
