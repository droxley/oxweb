2020's Poem

The Purloined Year

Once upon an annus dreary, COVID masks would make us weary;
altering international commerce as nothing had before.
Strange hours kept working from home, catching colleagues only by phone;
always staring, sometimes glaring ever trapped by our home decor.
What is one more month or four?

Authorities sometimes chasing, struggling with contract tracing;
our well-being, items that freeing would be left at our front door.
The curve was all ours to flatten, deliv'ry our waists did fatten;
so much testing always vexing nasal swabs our poor brains did score.
Please, ah sneeze, please no encore!

The top man was not pastoral, a remedy electoral;
many promoters to their voters, off their butts they would implore.
All that night there was much counting, tension o'er the week was mounting;
first one state, the left elate, many on all sides in uproar.
Oration nothing like "four score".

And now the end is upon us, for a vaccine there is great fuss;
Christmas tiding brings distance subsiding, frequenting a bookstore.
But now the year is behind me, with no sense of melancholy;
it will forever, be my endeavour, to remember its lore.
Speak "2020" nevermore.
